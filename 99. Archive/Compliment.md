### Appearance

- "You look gorgeous."
- "You're stunning."
- "You look amazing today."
- "You're looking really good."
- "You're absolutely glowing."
- "You're fine as hell." _(very flirty; use only if you're already comfortable with each other)_

### Smile

- "Your smile is beautiful."
- "That smile is dangerous."
- "Your smile just made my day."
- "You've got the prettiest smile."

### Style

- "Your outfit is on point."
- "You've got great style."
- "You always know how to dress."
- "Your vibe is unmatched."

### Personality

- "You're genuinely fun to talk to."
- "You've got such good energy."
- "You're really smart."
- "I like your confidence."
- "You're one of the coolest people I've met."

### Gen Z / Slang

- "You're a whole vibe."
- "You're low-key gorgeous."
- "You're unreal."
- "You're literally glowing."
- "You're built different." _(usually about personality or talent)_
- "Sheesh... you look incredible."

### Flirty

- "How are you even this pretty?"
- "You're making it hard to focus."
- "You keep getting prettier every time I see you."
- "You're trouble." 😉
- "I think you just stole my attention."

### Avoid

These often come across as disrespectful or immature:

- "You're hot AF." _(unless you already have that kind of relationship)_
- "Damn girl, you're sexy."
- "You're my type fr." _(can feel generic)_
- Overusing "queen," "goddess," or excessive emojis if you don't know her well.

The best compliments are **specific**. Instead of just saying:

> "You're beautiful."

Say:

> "I really like your smile—it looks so genuine."

Or:

> "Your style is really unique. It suits you."
