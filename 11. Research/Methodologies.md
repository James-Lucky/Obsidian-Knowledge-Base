#### **1. Definition of Data**

- Data is defined as the raw facts, figures, and information collected for the purpose of analysis.
    
- It serves as the foundation upon which researchers build their arguments, test hypotheses, and reach conclusions.
    

#### **2. Types of Data**

The video categorizes data into two primary types based on their source:

- **Primary Data:** Data collected for the first time by the researcher specifically for the current project. This is "fresh" information gathered through methods like surveys, interviews, or experiments.
    
- **Secondary Data:** Data that has already been collected by someone else for a different purpose but is reused by the researcher. Examples include government reports, census data, previous academic studies, and books.
    

#### **3. Qualitative vs. Quantitative Data**

The video also distinguishes between the nature of the data collected:

- **Quantitative Data:** Numerical information that can be measured and analyzed statistically (e.g., age, weight, test scores).
    
- **Qualitative Data:** Descriptive, non-numerical information that captures qualities or characteristics (e.g., opinions, feelings, or observations).
    

#### **4. Importance of Data Collection**

- **Reliability:** Choosing the right data collection method ensures the results are accurate.
    
- **Validity:** The data must directly relate to the research question to provide a valid answer
