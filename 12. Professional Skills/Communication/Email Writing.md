### 1. Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Poor Formatting:** Avoid incorrect capital casing (always use Title Case for names and organizations) and insufficient line breaks. Split long text into short paragraphs for readability.
- **Improper Punctuation:** Never add spaces before commas or periods; always add a space after them.
- **AI Red Flags:** If using AI, ensure you remove placeholder brackets (e.g., `[Company Name]`) to avoid looking careless.
- **Lack of Proof:** Asking for an opportunity without providing links to previous work or a portfolio is a major mistake.

### 2. The BRACE Framework

This framework is designed to help you write high-conversion emails:

- **B - Beginning:** Start by clearly stating who you are, your core skills, location, and years of experience. Keep it concise (e.g., 165 characters instead of 700+) .
- **R - Reveal Your Offer:** Clearly state what you want and why it is valuable to the reader. Use industry-specific references to show expertise.
- **A - Authenticate:** Provide social proof. Use hyperlinks that include success metrics (e.g., "Edited this video which gained 1M views") rather than simple link trees.
- **C - Close:** End with a professional email signature including your full name, phone number, and portfolio links.
- **E - Elevate:** Use a professional, front-facing profile picture (DP) and a professional email domain (e.g., `admin@yourname.com`) instead of a generic Gmail address.

### 4. Strategy & Follow-Up

- **Targeting:** Don't just email CEOs. Target HRs, creative heads, or senior team members in the department you want to join.
- **Follow-Up Rule:** Most people don't reply to the first email. Follow up after **3 days**, but never more than twice to avoid being blocked.
- **Thread Consistency:** Always reply to your original email thread rather than starting a new one; it shows consistency.
