# Move

- **Move out** — Leave a place where you live.
    - _She moved out of her apartment last week._
- **Move in** — Start living in a new place.
    - _They moved into a new house yesterday._
- **Move away** — Go to live somewhere else, usually far away.
    - _He moved away to Canada._
- **Move along** — Continue moving; don't stop.
    - _The police told the crowd to move along._
- **Move on** — Continue to the next thing; recover from something.
    - _Let's move on to the next topic._
    - _It's time to move on after the breakup._
- **Move over** — Shift to make space for someone or something.
    - _Move over so I can sit down._
- **Move up** — Go to a higher position, level, or rank.
    - _She moved up to manager._

# Play

- **Play about** _(or play around)_ — Fool around; behave in a silly or careless way.
    - _The kids were playing about in the garden._
- **Play upon** — Take advantage of someone's emotions or weaknesses.
    - _The scammer played upon their fears._
- **Play on** — Exploit someone's feelings or continue happening.
    - _He played on her sympathy._
    - _The movie played on for another hour._
- **Play along** — Cooperate or pretend to agree with a plan or joke.
    - _I knew it was a prank, but I played along._
- **Play back** — Replay recorded audio or video.
    - _Play back the recording._
- **Play at** — Pretend to do something; do something without being serious.
    - _The children played at being doctors._
- **Play down** — Make something seem less important or serious.
    - _The company played down the problem._
- **Play up** — Emphasize something; or (British English) behave badly / stop working properly.
    - _The advertisement played up the product's features._
    - _My knee is playing up again._
- **Play out** — Happen until the end; unfold.
    - _Let's see how the situation plays out._
- **Play with** — Handle or experiment with something; tease or manipulate.
    - _She played with her hair._
    - _The scientist played with different ideas._
    - _Don't play with my emotions._
      
      # Love & Relationships

- **Blind by love** _(more common: **Blinded by love**)_ — So in love that you can't see someone's faults.
    - _He was blinded by love and ignored all the warning signs._
- **Head over heels in love** _(not "heads")_ — Deeply and completely in love.
    - _She's head over heels in love with him._
- **Under someone's spell** — Strongly attracted or influenced by someone.
    - _He was completely under her spell._
- **Smitten with someone** — Suddenly and deeply attracted to someone.
    - _She is smitten with her new classmate._
- **Lovey-dovey** — Showing a lot of affection in a cheesy or overly romantic way.
    - _They were being all lovey-dovey at the party._
- **Split up** — End a romantic relationship.
    - _They split up after five years together._
- **Fancy someone** _(British English)_ — Be romantically attracted to someone.
    - _I think she fancies him._
- **Love someone with all your heart and soul** _(more common than "with heart and soul")_ — Love someone completely and sincerely.
    - _He loves his family with all his heart and soul._
- **Love rat** _(not "kove rat")_ — A person who cheats on their romantic partner.
    - _She found out he was a love rat._
- **A match made in heaven** — Two people who are perfect for each other.
    - _Everyone says they're a match made in heaven._
- **Carry a torch for someone** _(not "tourch")_ — Continue to have romantic feelings for someone, often unreturned.
    - _He's still carrying a torch for his ex._
- **Kiss and make up** _(not "pu")_ — Become friends again after an argument.
    - _They argued yesterday but kissed and made up today._
- **Tie the knot** — Get married.
    - _They plan to tie the knot next summer._
