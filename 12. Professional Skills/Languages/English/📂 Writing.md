# 🔗 Better Connectors

Use these words to connect ideas more naturally in writing and formal speaking.

## Instead of **So**

| Better Word | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| Therefore | As a result | He worked hard; therefore, he succeeded. |
| Thus | In this way / As a result | The weather was bad; thus, the match was cancelled. |
| Consequently | Because of that | She missed the train; consequently, she arrived late. |

---

## Instead of **But**

| Better Word | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| However | Despite that | The task was difficult; however, we completed it. |
| Nevertheless | Even so | He was tired; nevertheless, he kept working. |

---

## Instead of **Because**

| Better Word | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| Since | Because | Since it was raining, we stayed home. |
| As | Because | As I was busy, I couldn't attend. |

---

## Instead of **And**

| Better Word | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| Moreover | In addition | She is talented; moreover, she is hardworking. |
| Furthermore | In addition | Furthermore, the report includes new data. |
| In Addition | Also | In addition, we need more volunteers. |

---

## Instead of **Or**

| Better Word | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| Otherwise | If not | Hurry up; otherwise, you'll miss the train. |
| Alternatively | Another option | Alternatively, you can contact us by email. |

---

## Giving Examples

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Such as | For example | Programming languages such as Python and Java are popular. |

# 📚 Better Vocabulary

Replace common words with stronger alternatives.

| Basic Word | Better Word | Example |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| Get | Obtain | You must obtain permission first. |
| Help | Assist | She assisted me with the project. |
| Buy | Purchase | We purchased a new laptop. |
| Show | Demonstrate | He demonstrated the new feature. |
| Start | Commence | The meeting will commence at 10 AM. |
| End | Conclude | The seminar concluded successfully. |
| Try | Attempt | He attempted to solve the problem. |
| Tell | Inform | Please inform me about any changes. |
| Need | Require | This task requires more time. |
| Give | Provide | The company provides free training. |
| Fix | Resolve | We resolved the issue quickly. |
| Use | Utilize | Utilize your resources wisely. |
| Ask | Inquire | Please inquire about the schedule. |
| Talk | Converse | They conversed for hours. |
| Think | Consider | Please consider my suggestion. |

> **Tip:** Use these words in formal writing, business communication, and presentations. In casual conversations, simpler words often sound more natural.

# 💼 Formal English

These words make your English sound more polite and professional.

| Casual | Formal | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Sorry | Apologies | Apologies for the inconvenience. |
| Maybe | Perhaps | Perhaps we should discuss this tomorrow. |
| Hi | Greetings | Greetings, everyone. |
| Yeah | Indeed | Indeed, that's a great idea. |

# 🏢 Professional Workplace English

## Asking for a Reply

| Casual | Professional |
|---------|--------------|
| Reply soon. | Kindly respond at your earliest convenience. |

**Example**

> Kindly respond at your earliest convenience regarding the project update.

---

## Asking for Help

| Casual | Professional |
|---------|--------------|
| I need help. | I would appreciate your assistance. |

**Example**

> I would appreciate your assistance with this report.

---

## Promising Completion

| Casual | Professional |
|---------|--------------|
| I'll do it soon. | I will complete it shortly. |

**Example**

> I will complete the documentation shortly.

---

## Requesting Information

| Casual | Professional |
|---------|--------------|
| Tell me. | Kindly inform me. |

**Example**

> Kindly inform me if there are any updates.

---
