# 1. Modals

**Modals** are auxiliary (helping) verbs used to express ability, permission, possibility, advice, obligation, necessity, etc.

---

## 1. Can

**Meaning:** सकना

### Uses

- Ability
- Informal permission

### Structure

> Subject + can + V₁

### Examples

- Can I get the job?
- Can I earn money?
- She can speak English.
- Can I use your pen?

---

## 2. May

**Meaning:** सकता/सकती हूँ (Formal)

### Uses

- Formal permission

### Structure

> Subject + may + V₁

### Example

- May I take this coffee?
- May I come in?

---

## 3. Could

**Meaning:** सकता था / सकती थी

### Uses

- Past ability
- Polite request
- Possibility

### Structure

> Subject + could + V₁

### Example

- I could not understand his point.
- Could you help me?

---

## 4. Might

**Meaning:** शायद / संभवतः

### Uses

- Possibility
- Suggestion (less certain)

### Structure

> Subject + might + V₁

### Examples

- I might go with you. _(Possibility)_
- You might visit the garden. _(Suggestion)_

---

## 5. Should

**Meaning:** चाहिए

### Uses

- Advice
- Probability

### Structure

> Subject + should + V₁

### Examples

- He should learn ML.
- He should be here soon.

---

## 6. Must

### Uses

- Obligation
- Moral duty
- Strong recommendation
- Logical conclusion / certainty

### Structure

> Subject + must + V₁

### Examples

- You must go to sleep now.
- You must keep the rules in your mind.

---

## 7. Ought to

**Meaning:** चाहिए

### Uses

- Formal advice
- Ideal or morally correct action

### Structure

> Subject + ought to + V₁

### Example

- You ought to come on time.

---

## 8. Has to / Have to / Had to / Will have to

### Uses

Used when there is **no choice** or **necessity**.

### Tense Forms

|Tense|Form|
|---|---|
|Present|Has to / Have to|
|Past|Had to|
|Future|Will have to|

### Structure

> Subject + has/have to + V₁

### Examples

- He has to learn JS.
- I have to finish my work.
- She had to leave early.
- We will have to wait.

---

## 9. Need

### Uses

- Requirement
- Necessity

### Structure

> Subject + need to + V₁

### Examples

- I need to study.
- You need to practice every day.

---

## 10. Would

### Uses

- Polite requests
- Offering something
- Past habits

### Structure

> Subject + would + V₁

### Examples

- Would you like to say something?
- Would you like some coffee?
- When I was a child, I would play outside every evening.

---

# Indefinite Words

These words are used to mean **"no matter..."** or **"any..."**.

|Word|Meaning (Hindi)|
|---|---|
|Whatever|कुछ भी|
|Whenever|कभी भी|
|Wherever|कहीं भी|
|Whichever|जो भी|
|Whoever|कोई भी|
|However|कैसे भी / कोई फर्क नहीं पड़ता|

### Examples

- Whatever happens, stay calm.
- Whenever you need help, call me.
- Wherever you go, be careful.
- Whichever book you choose is fine.
- Whoever comes first will win.
- However difficult it is, never give up.
