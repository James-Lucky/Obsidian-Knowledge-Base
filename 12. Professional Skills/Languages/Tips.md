# 1. Pronunciation Hacks

- **Break words into chunks** — Divide long words into smaller parts to pronounce them more easily.
    - _Example:_ **in-for-ma-tion**, **re-spon-si-bi-li-ty**
- **Record yourself and listen** — Record your pronunciation, compare it with a native speaker, and identify mistakes.
- **Shadowing with subtitles** — Listen to a native speaker and repeat immediately, matching their pronunciation, rhythm, and intonation while using subtitles.
- **Focus on stressed syllables** — Identify the stressed syllable, **stretch the stressed vowel slightly**, and **raise your pitch** on that vowel.
    - _Example:_ **TA**-ble, com-**PU**-ter, pho-to-**GRA**-phy
- **Practice problem sounds first** — Work on sounds you find difficult before practicing full words or sentences.
    - _Examples:_ **/θ/** (_think_), **/ð/** (_this_), **/v/** vs **/w/**, **/r/** vs **/l/**
- **Use tongue twisters** — Repeat challenging phrases to improve clarity, speed, and mouth coordination.
    - _Example:_ _She sells seashells by the seashore._
    - _Example:_ _Red lorry, yellow lorry._


## 2. Improve Writing Skills

## 1. Improve Grammar & Vocabulary

- Learn grammar rules and apply them consistently.
- Expand your vocabulary daily.
- Prefer precise words over complex words.
- Avoid repeating the same words.

---

## 2. Choose the Right Vocabulary & Writing Style

- Match your vocabulary to your audience.
- Maintain a coherent and consistent writing style.
- Avoid unnecessary jargon.
- Prefer clarity over sophistication.

---

## 3. Use the Appropriate Tone

- **Formal** – Academic papers, reports, business communication.
- **Semi-formal** – Professional emails, LinkedIn posts, blogs.
- **Informal** – Friends, personal messages, casual writing.

---

## 4. Show Emotion Through Strong Verbs & Adverbs

- Replace weak verbs with stronger ones.
- Use adverbs sparingly to add emphasis.
- Prefer showing rather than telling.

**Weak:** _He was very angry._

**Better:** _He slammed the door and stormed out._

---

## 5. Organize Ideas into Paragraphs

- One main idea per paragraph.
- Start with a clear topic sentence.
- Support it with explanations or examples.
- End with a transition to the next idea.

---

## 6. Write with an Evidence-Based Tone

- Support claims with facts, data, examples, or research.
- Avoid unsupported assumptions.
- Distinguish facts from opinions.

---

## 7. Be Clear and Concise

- Write short, direct sentences.
- Remove unnecessary words.
- Avoid filler phrases.
- Start with short paragraphs for better readability.

---

## 8. Improve Coherence & Flow

- Connect ideas logically.
- Use transition words naturally.

Examples:

- However
- Therefore
- Moreover
- In contrast
- As a result
- For example
- Consequently

---

## 9. Use Active Voice

**Passive:** _The report was written by John._

**Active:** _John wrote the report._

Active voice is usually clearer and more engaging.

---

## 10. Read Before You Write

- Read books, articles, essays, and high-quality blogs.
- Notice sentence structure, vocabulary, transitions, and tone.
- Copy good writing patterns instead of memorizing words.

---

## 11. Rewrite and Edit

The first draft is rarely the best.

Check for:

- Grammar
- Clarity
- Repetition
- Word choice
- Sentence length
- Logical flow

---

## 12. Get Feedback

- Use AI or grammar checkers.
- Ask others to review your writing.
- Compare your original and revised versions to identify recurring mistakes.

---

## 13. Practice Different Types of Writing

- Essays
- Emails
- Technical documentation
- Blog posts
- Stories
- Product descriptions
- Social media posts
- Research summaries

Each type improves a different writing skill.

---

## 14. Think Before You Write

Before writing, ask:

- Who is my audience?
- What is my goal?
- What is the key message?
- What action should the reader take?

---

## 15. Read Aloud

Reading aloud helps you catch:

- Awkward phrasing
- Long sentences
- Repetition
- Grammar mistakes
- Unnatural flow

---

# Daily Writing Practice (15–20 Minutes)

- Read for **10 minutes**.
- Learn **5–10 new words** and use each in a sentence.
- Write **150–300 words** on any topic.
- Edit your writing after 30–60 minutes.
- Compare your version with AI suggestions.
- Rewrite the piece using the feedback.
- Keep a "mistake journal" to track recurring grammar and vocabulary errors.
