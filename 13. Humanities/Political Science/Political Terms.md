| Term                  | Meaning                                                                            |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Condescending**     | Talking to someone as if they are less intelligent or inferior.                    |
| **Dogmatism**         | Refusing to change beliefs despite evidence or arguments.                          |
| **Authoritarianism**  | Supporting strict obedience to authority and limiting freedoms.                    |
| **Totalitarianism**   | A system where the state controls almost every aspect of life.                     |
| **Populism**          | Appealing to ordinary people, often against elites.                                |
| **Nationalism**       | Strong identification with and support for one's nation.                           |
| **Patriotism**        | Love and support for one's country.                                                |
| **Imperialism**       | Extending a country's power through conquest or influence.                         |
| **Colonialism**       | Establishing control over another country or territory.                            |
| **Fascism**           | An authoritarian political ideology emphasizing nationalism and centralized power. |
| **Communism**         | A system where property is collectively owned and social classes are abolished.    |
| **Socialism**         | Advocates collective or government ownership/control of certain resources.         |
| **Capitalism**        | An economic system based on private ownership and free markets.                    |
| **Liberalism**        | Supports individual rights, democracy, and political freedoms.                     |
| **Conservatism**      | Favors tradition, stability, and gradual change.                                   |
| **Libertarianism**    | Supports maximum individual liberty and minimal government intervention.           |
| **Anarchism**         | Advocates for the absence of government or hierarchical authority.                 |
| **Oligarchy**         | Rule by a small group of powerful individuals.                                     |
| **Plutocracy**        | Rule by the wealthy.                                                               |
| **Demagoguery**       | Gaining support by appealing to emotions and prejudices rather than reason.        |
| **Propaganda**        | Information used to influence opinions, often in a biased manner.                  |
| **Polarization**      | Division of society into opposing groups with little middle ground.                |
| **Censorship**        | Suppressing or restricting information or speech.                                  |
| **Xenophobia**        | Fear or dislike of foreigners or outsiders.                                        |
| **Ethnocentrism**     | Judging other cultures by the standards of one's own culture.                      |
| **Nepotism**          | Favoring relatives for jobs or positions of power.                                 |
| **Cronyism**          | Favoring friends or close associates, especially in politics.                      |
| **Bureaucracy**       | A system governed by rules, procedures, and administrative officials.              |
| **Diplomacy**         | Managing relations between countries through negotiation.                          |
| **Sovereignty**       | A state's authority to govern itself independently.                                |
| **Hegemony**          | Dominance of one state or group over others.                                       |
| **Appeasement**       | Avoiding conflict by giving concessions to an aggressor.                           |
| **Meritocracy**       | A system where people advance based on ability and achievement.                    |
| **Radicalism**        | Supporting significant or revolutionary changes in society or politics.            |
| **Moderation**        | Avoiding extreme views and seeking balanced positions.                             |
| **Ideology**          | A set of beliefs or principles that guide political or social actions.             |
| **Partisanship**      | Strong support for a particular political party or cause.                          |
| **Hypocrisy**         | Claiming to have moral standards while acting contrary to them.                    |
| **Strawman Argument** | Misrepresenting someone's argument to make it easier to attack.                    |
| **Ad Hominem**        | Attacking the person instead of addressing their argument.                         |
| **Whataboutism**      | Responding to criticism by pointing to someone else's faults instead.              |
