Governments are systems of rule that organize and exercise power over a society, ranging from participatory systems to autocratic regimes. Democratic systems, such as **Republics** and **Constitutional Monarchies**, rely on the consent of the governed through elections to limit the authority of leaders, whereas **Dictatorships** and **Totalitarian States** concentrate absolute power in a single person or political party without public mandate. Other variations include **Oligarchies** and **Plutocracies**, where power is held by a small elite or the wealthy, and **Federal** systems, which distribute decision-making authority between central and local governments. At the extremes, a **Communist** state exerts centralized control over the economy and social services, while **Anarchy** represents a state of total absence of government, and **Transitional** governments mark periods of unstable shift between these various political structures.

1. **Federal:** A central government shares power with a number of small local governments.
    
2. **Democracy:** Where the government is elected by the people.
    
3. **Plutocracy:** A government composed of the wealthy class.
    
4. **Oligarchy:** Rule by a small group of people who share similar interests or family relations.
    
5. **Capitalist:** People can own their own businesses and property but usually pay taxes from some of the money they make. Most capitalist governments provide their own education, health, and welfare services.
    
6. **Monarchy:** Rule by an individual (King or Queen) who has inherited the role and expects to pass it onto their heir.
    
7. **Regional or Local:** A government or council that controls a smaller area within a country. Most local governments are controlled by the central government.
    
8. **Communist:** The government owns businesses and farms. It provides healthcare, education, and welfare.
    
9. **Constitutional Monarchy:** Has a democratic government that limits the control of the Monarch (King or Queen).
    
10. **Transitional:** A country that is changing from one type of government to another.
    
11. **Despotism:** Rule by a single leader; all his or her subjects are considered his or her slaves.
    
12. **Totalitarian State:** A country with one political party. (e.g., In England there are three main parties: Labour, Conservative, and Liberal Democrats).

13. **Revolutionary:** If a government is overthrown by force, the overthrowing government is sometimes called the Revolutionary Government.
    
14. **Anarchy:** A situation where there is no government.
    
15. **Republic:** A country that has no monarch. The head of the country is usually an elected president.
    
16. **Dictatorship:** A country ruled by a single leader who has full power over the country. This leader is not elected.
