### Daily Practices

- [[00:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=32)] **Push to GitHub every night:** Summarizing your daily work in a commit message builds an undeniable proof of work and provides a solid dopamine hit to end the day.
    
- [[01:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=66)] **Read code from someone smarter:** Spend 5 minutes daily reading professional, production-level code (like Shadcn for React or FastAPI for Python) to learn syntax, naming conventions, and structure.
    
- [[04:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=247)] **Maintain a `til.md` file:** Keep a simple "Today I Learned" document. Forcing yourself to condense a new topic into one line translates thoughts to words and solidifies long-term memory.
    
- [[04:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=285)] **Keep your phone in another room:** Having your phone nearby puts a permanent drain on your willpower and focus. Make accessing it as difficult as possible during work sessions.
    

### Mindset & Learning

- [[01:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=103)] **Build impossible-sounding projects:** Stop building only what you already know how to build (like simple CRUD apps). Tackle projects where you have no idea where to start—following the documentation step-by-step teaches you significantly more.
    
- [[02:17](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=137)] **Discuss problems with others:** Explaining a problem to someone else often helps you find logical holes in your own thinking. Reviewing code with peers builds a sense of community.
    
- [[03:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=218)] **Explain concepts to juniors:** If you can't simplify a topic, you don't understand it deeply. Mentally preparing a "TED Talk" on new concepts drastically improves retention.
    
- [[04:57](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=297)] **Build dissatisfaction:** Don't settle for code that just "works." Cultivate a curiosity to understand the internals and under-the-hood operations of the tools and frameworks you rely on.
    
- [[05:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=351)] **Build what you actually care about:** Combine programming with your hobbies (sports, anime, music). Treating coding strictly as a money-making tool leads to burnout, but building fun tools keeps you engaged.
    
- [[08:56](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=536)] **Don't compare your stats:** Everyone has different starting environments and circumstances. Focus solely on raising your own skill cap instead of comparing your journey to others.
    

### Workflow Optimization

- [[03:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=181)] **Use Vim motions:** Learning keyboard navigation shortcuts gamifies the coding process, saves mental load, and vastly increases typing speed once you get past the initial 15-minute learning curve.
    
- [[06:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=391)] **Listen to calm music:** Non-lyrical, calming music lowers cortisol and clears background mental processes, acting as a streamlined focus tool (especially beneficial for ADHD).
    
- [[07:07](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=427)] **Plan like a senior engineer:** Spend 80% of your time planning (architecture, schemas, API endpoints) and 20% coding. This prevents mid-session overthinking and messy refactoring.
    
- [[07:44](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=464)] **Prioritize sleep:** Pulling all-nighters impairs performance similarly to being legally drunk. Sleep is when your brain consolidates information from short-term to long-term memory.
    
- [[08:39](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z36-xqUv3W8&t=519)] **Get a good keyboard:** A tactile, satisfying typing experience makes the physical act of coding addictive and enjoyable.
